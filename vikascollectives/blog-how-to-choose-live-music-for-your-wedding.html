How to Choose Live Music for Your Wedding

Solo saxophonist or full band? A performer’s practical guide from hundreds of weddings across India and abroad.

I have played at hundreds of weddings — in Delhi banquet halls, Rajasthan palaces, Goa beaches and destination venues from Dubai to Australia. The couples who got their music right all made the same few decisions early. Here is the guide I wish every couple had.

1. Match the format to the moment

A wedding is not one event; it is five or six moments with different energy. Solo saxophone is unbeatable for bridal entries, varmala and cocktail hours — elegant, emotional, and it never drowns conversation. A full band belongs at the sangeet and after-party, where you want volume and dance energy. Many couples book both: solo sax for the ceremony, band for the celebration.

2. Decide where music matters most

If the budget forces a choice, spend on the moments guests will remember: the entry, the varmala, and the first thirty minutes of cocktails when the room fills. Background dinner music can run on a playlist; a bridal entry cannot.

3. Brief your musician properly

Share three things with any live performer: the schedule (with realistic Indian-wedding delays built in), five songs you love, and one song you never want to hear. A professional builds the rest around that. Ask whether they curate set lists per event or play the same show everywhere — the difference is enormous.

4. Check the practical details

Confirm who provides sound equipment, how much setup time is needed, and whether the performer has played your venue type before. An open lawn, a marble banquet hall and a beach each need different sound handling — experience here is what separates a smooth evening from a stressful one.

5. Trust live over recorded for the peaks

Guests forget playlists. They remember the moment a live saxophone began playing as the bride appeared. After thirty years, I can promise you: that moment is worth every rupee.
