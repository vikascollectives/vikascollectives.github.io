Alto vs Tenor Saxophone: Which Should a Beginner Buy?

An honest comparison of size, sound, cost and playability — from a performer and teacher of 20+ years.

It is the first question every new student asks me, and after 20+ years of teaching I give the same honest answer: for most beginners, start on alto — but here is the full picture so you can decide for yourself.

Size and physical comfort

The alto is smaller, lighter and needs less air. Children, teenagers and most adults find it far more comfortable for the first year. The tenor is larger and heavier, demands more breath support, and can tire a new player quickly — which quietly kills motivation.

Sound and repertoire

The alto has a bright, singing voice that suits Bollywood melodies and most film music beautifully — which is why it is my own primary instrument. The tenor has a deeper, breathier tone that many associate with classic jazz. If your dream is smoky jazz standards, the tenor's voice may call to you; if you want to play the songs you hear at Indian weddings and on the radio, the alto fits naturally.

Cost and practicality

Beginner alto saxophones are noticeably cheaper than tenors, and reeds and accessories cost less too. Altos are also easier to carry to lessons — a real factor for school students and commuting professionals.

The honest recommendation

Start on alto unless you have a strong, specific pull toward the tenor sound and the physical build to support it. Every technique you learn transfers between the two — many of my students add a tenor in their second or third year, and the switch takes weeks, not months.

Before you buy anything

Do not purchase an instrument before speaking with a teacher. I guide every new student on choosing a reliable beginner saxophone within their budget — the wrong instrument is the most expensive mistake a beginner can make.
